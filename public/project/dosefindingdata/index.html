Real data on lots of dose-finding trials

Last updated on May 9, 2020
Code Follow
Photo by Kristian Brock on Unsplash

Do we expect the incidence of dose-limiting toxicity to increase as dose increases in dose-escalation clinical trials? Categorically, yes.

Do we expect efficacy to be greater at higher doses? Implicitly yes - it is a reqirement of MTD-seeking methods like 3+3 and CRM.

But how well are each of these assumptions borne out by the data in real clinical trials? These questions motivated us to collect dose-level outcomes from recent dose-escalation clinical trials in cancer.

The dataset is introduced in this post. Analyses of the data will be conducted in future posts.
